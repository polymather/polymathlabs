What are Bootcamps

A developer bootcamp is an educational workshop intended to turn novices into functional programmers, no pun intended (Don't worry, you'll get it later). Bootcamps are led by expert developers and normally run from 8-12 weeks, where attendees focus on practical, real-world skills that will aide them in finding employment upon completion.

Benefits of Bootcamps

The most highly-regarded bootcamps focus on current techonologies, so attendees gain a skill-set that will be relevant and marketable to employers. Attendees learn to code while working on projects in a collaborative setting with other developers. This is designed to mimic how developers normally work in the real-world -- on teams.

Bootcamp Drawbacks

After the smartphone became a thing, bootcamps sprang up like wildfire. Many bootcamp alum have changed their lives by becoming developers, but thousands have left bootcamps and floundered, unable to find employment or advance their practice. See, employers know that just because Joe Schmoe attends a bootcamp, does not a developer make.

How to Attend

Pre-registration is only the beginning

1. Review the Checklist

So you've had a look at the dates and locations. Now, compare and contrast how the dates sync with your personal schedule. Once you feel comfortable that you can commit to the bootcamp, click the 'Pre-register Now!' button at the bottom of the page to launch the pre-registration form. On to step 2!

2. Enter Required Info

Take care to fill out required information on the form. Your first name, last name, and email address are necessary to move forward with registration. All correspondence before your bootcamp start date will be done via email, and if the information we have is incorrect, there's not a lot we can do to help.

3. Choose Preferred Session

The moment of truth has arrived. This is where you decide which day you will be devoting 3 hours of your life to for the next 12 weeks. You will have the option of choosing from 7 sessions and three different locations. Keep in mind that registration preference will be given to those who pay in full.

4. Choose Alternate Session

The alternate session will be your second-most preferred session. Think of it as fallback that is not what you wanted, but still works with your schedule. As much as possible will be done to accomodate your first choice, but having a second option is never a bad idea.

5. Choose Preferred Location

The option is provided to choose a preferred location. This matters because demand will drive bootcamp locations. If the Decatur bootcamp is not filled with the minimum number of attendees, bootcamp will not be held there. This means your preferred location is not guaranteed, but you may still reserve a spot at another location by completing registration.

6. Enter Referral

If applicable, you can enter the name of the registrant who referred you to our bootcamp. He or she will receive a $300 rebate upon course completion. Each registrant is eligible for only one referral per bootcamp. To qualify, your referral will need to correctly enter your first and last names on the form. Make sure they get it right!
